# Interview Question Bank

Pick from these — don't ask all of them. The goal is to learn what you don't already know
from the request or from screenshot analysis. Ask one topic at a time. Prefer the
structured multiple-choice tool when the options are discrete; use plain text for
open-ended taste questions where you want the user's own words.

## Screenshot pre-hook (only when screenshots were shared)

- **Is this a reference you admire, or your current UI you want to improve?** (the branch
  everything depends on — ask first)
- If reference: what specifically pulls you in here — the type, the color, the layout, the
  feeling? What would you change if it were yours?
- If current UI: what's not working? What made you want to redesign now?
- Are there parts of this you want to keep no matter what (a logo, a color, a layout)?
- How close to this should the result be — same family, inspired-by, or just a starting point?

## Subject (always nail this down)

- In one sentence, what is this product/site and who is it for?
- What's the single most important thing a visitor should do or feel on this page?
- What's the one thing about this product that's genuinely different from the alternatives?
- Is there real content/copy yet, or should the brief assume placeholder?

## Personality

- Pick 2–3 adjectives for how it should feel. (offer a spread: calm, playful, premium,
  serious, raw/honest, technical, warm, bold, editorial, friendly, luxurious, minimal)
- Who's the closest "if our brand were a [magazine / car / store], it'd be ___"?
- What feeling do you want to avoid at all costs? (sterile, corporate, childish, trendy…)

## Density & layout

- Minimal and spacious, or rich and maximalist? (a slider in your head: 1–5)
- Content-heavy (lots to show) or focused (one clear message)?
- Any layout you already have in mind — single scroll, dashboard, grid, long-form?

## Color

- Any brand colors you already own or must use?
- Light, dark, or either?
- Colors to avoid (competitor colors, ones you dislike)?
- Bold/saturated or muted/restrained?

## Typography

- Characterful and expressive type, or quiet and neutral?
- Modern (geometric/sans) or classic (serif/traditional) leaning?
- Any fonts you love or have licensed already?

## Motion & animation

- Motion appetite: none / subtle polish (hovers, fades) / one signature animated moment /
  motion-forward throughout?
- Anything specific you've seen and loved — scroll reveals, a hero animation, micro-interactions?
- Any reason to keep motion minimal (performance, reduced-motion users, conservative audience)?

## References & anti-references

- 1–3 sites/apps you love the look of (even outside your industry)?
- 1–3 you actively dislike — these are often more useful than the loves.
- Any competitor sites the result should *not* look like?

## Hard constraints

- Existing brand guide, design system, or component library to respect?
- Framework/tech the build must use (affects what's realistic)?
- Accessibility requirements beyond the baseline (specific contrast, audience needs)?
- Anything that must appear on the page (legal, logos, specific sections)?
